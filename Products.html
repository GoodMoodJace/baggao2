Bagwea(Grass to Bags)

It is endowed with indigenous raw materials such as pattaga, sarakat, and pandan which are proven to be good raw materials for bayong. At first, the residents regarded these raw materials as agricultural wastes. They are taken for granted, and are burned and left to rot on the fields. Also, residents are reluctant to transform these raw materials into bayong and other woven products because of the absence of technical know-how or skills in weaving.,

Crops and Rice

Baggao, as is true to the whole province of Cagayan, is primarily an agricultural community. This is shown by the fact that majority of the population depends upon agriculture for their livelihood and income,

Tobacco

Tobacco grower Pablito Batara improves productivity by integrating vegetable farming in tobacco production in his farm in Masical, Baggao, Cagayan. According to NTA Cagayan Branch Manager Corazon R. Riazonda, Batara was a beneficiary of the NTA Vegetable Seeds Distribution project last year, and one of the 52 tobacco-corn farmers who comprise the Block Farm in Masical, one of the two block farm sites in Cagayan.

Yellow Corn

Baggao and Gattaran, Cagayan’s neighboring second- and third-largest municipalities in terms of land area, are the top corn-producing towns of the province, which accounts for nearly 25 percent (and 90-95% of its Bt corn) of Region 2’s total corn output. It ranks next to the top in regional (and national) production, Isabela Province. Cagayan stands to boost its Bt corn production because farmers are opening up more hilly lands to cultivate. Its five top corn-producing towns—Baggao, Gattaran, Peñablanca, Alcala, and Amulung—all have extensive uplands, with the first three municipalities’ areas extending to the Sierra Madre Mountain Range.,
